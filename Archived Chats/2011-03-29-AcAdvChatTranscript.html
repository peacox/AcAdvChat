First Question
7:06 pm AcAdvChat: MOD: Q1: Often students don't understand what an 'elective' means. How do you explain it? #AcAdv
7:07 pm jmenglund03: Hi everyone, Jennifer Englund #sagrad at #umn #acadv
7:08 pm howardsj: @AcAdvChat Q1: I explain them as courses not used to fulfill any major or gen.ed requirements that the student gets to pick #acadv
7:08 pm bradpopiolek: Q1: Elective = small piece of pie compared to gen ed, major, & minor. Pursue intro course in area of personal interest #acadv
7:08 pm gregsobie: Q1: I just tell them that and elective is just a course they get to pick! Whatever it is they'd like to learn about is fine. #acadv
7:08 pm braeingle: Q1 It depends upon type of elective: social science, gen ed, distributive, or required. #acadv
7:09 pm brianbuckwald: Q1: Explain elective is another way to say choice. There are many "choice points" or "customization opportunities" to a degree. #acadv
7:09 pm dawntraynor: Q1: courses that offer an opportunity to explore, or do something outside of your required courses that interests you #acadv
7:10 pm howardsj: @AcAdvChat Q1: Can be an opportunity to enhance major or to explore other areas of interest. #acadv
7:10 pm gregsobie: @brianbuckwald I like the customization opportunities idea. #acadv
7:11 pm Karen_Higgs: Q1: Unrelated to major program of study but, if chosen wisely, enhances academic experience & chance to explore subj of interest #acadv
7:11 pm dawntraynor: Q1: (contd) at USC many of our majors actually incorporate "major electives" as well, so we have to be careful with wording sometimes #acadv
7:11 pm howardsj: @brianbuckwald Ha... "customization options" :) I like it #acadv
